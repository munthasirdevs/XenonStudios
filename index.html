Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Xenon Studios different from a traditional digital agency?

+
We identify as a "Digital Foundry" rather than a traditional agency. We specialize in building "cinematic" digital identities and high-performance web systems that go beyond standard templates. Our focus is on immersive UI/UX, motion logic, and engineering-heavy solutions like private LLM integration and cloud-native architectures, designed specifically for brands that want a futuristic, high-concurrency edge.

Which industries do you specialize in?

+
While we are a global agency serving clients in over 5 countries, our core expertise lies in the SaaS (Software as a Service) and Enterprise sectors. We thrive on projects that require technical precision—such as fintech platforms, AI-driven applications, and brands that need a high-fidelity, interactive web presence to stand out in competitive markets.

How do you handle security for high-performance systems?

+
Security is built into our foundation, not added as an afterthought. We implement Hardened Security protocols, including end-to-end data encryption and audit-ready standards. For clients integrating AI, we offer the deployment of private LLMs to ensure that sensitive data remains within a secure, controlled environment rather than being processed by public third-party servers.

How long does a typical "Next-Gen" project take to deploy?

+
Because we prioritize technical excellence and custom engineering, timelines vary by complexity. A standard high-performance website usually takes 4 to 8 weeks, while comprehensive, custom-engineered web applications or enterprise-level architectures can take between 3 to 6 months. We provide a detailed deployment log and roadmap during the discovery phase to ensure total transparency.

What is your "Cinematic UI/UX" approach?

+
Our "Cinematic" approach treats a website like a film experience. We leverage advanced technologies like Three.js, WebGL, and GSAP to create 3D graphics and fluid animations. This ensures that every user interaction feels purposeful and high-end, moving away from static layouts to create "artifacts" that are visually stunning yet technically optimized for performance.
